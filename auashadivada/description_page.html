Tulsi

Discription

Plant Description Tulsi, also known as Holy Basil (Ocimum sanctum), is a sacred and medicinal plant native to India, revered for its spiritual and healing properties. Often referred to as the "Queen of Herbs," tulsi is a cornerstone of Ayurveda, known for its ability to boost immunity, reduce stress, and promote overall well-being. Its leaves are rich in antioxidants and essential oils, offering anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral benefits. Tulsi is commonly consumed as tea, used in remedies for colds, coughs, and respiratory issues, and valued for its ability to purify the air. Easy to grow, it thrives in sunlight and well-draining soil, making it a cherished addition to homes and gardens worldwide.

Preparing to Plant Tulsi

  1. Select Variety: Choose from Rama, Krishna, or Vana Tulsi.
  2. Location: Ensure 6-8 hours of sunlight and warm temperatures (70°F-95°F).
  3. Soil: Use well-draining, compost-enriched soil (pH 6.0-7.5).
  4. Pot (Optional): A 6-8 inch pot with drainage is ideal for container planting.
  5. Planting: Soak seeds or plant cuttings in moist soil.
  6. Watering: Keep soil moist; water less in winter.
  7. Fertilizing: Add compost every 4-6 weeks.
  8. Transplanting: Move 3-4 inch seedlings to larger pots or outdoors.
